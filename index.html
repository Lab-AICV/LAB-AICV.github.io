The Autonomous and Intelligent Vehicles Control Research Group focuses on design, control, and optimization of Mechatronics and Thermal systems. The main theme of our research focuses on developing advanced control and optimization techniques for Mechatronics, particularly Robotics (mobile robots, reconfigurable robot manipulators), Automotive (hybrid vehicles), and Thermal (thermoelectric/thermo-acoustic).

Director of the Autonomous and Intelligent Vehicles Control Research Group

Dr. Biglarbegian obtained his Ph.D. in Mechanical and Mechatronics Engineering, from the University of Waterloo, M.A.Sc. in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Toronto, and B.Sc. in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Tehran, respectively. He has been with the School of Engineering, the University of Guelph since 2011. He is a long-distance runner, enjoys playing tennis and skiing
